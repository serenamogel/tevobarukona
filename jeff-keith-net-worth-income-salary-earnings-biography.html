Jeff Keith was born in Idabel, Oklahoma, and attended Idabel High School before moving to Sacramento, California, with his father. Jeff founded the Troubleshooter band with Pete Pedri and his brother BK, and then joined the City Kidd in 1983. The band changed their name to Tesla in 1986 and released their debut studio album, “Mechanical Resonance,” which went platinum in the United States with over a million copies sold and peaked at No. 32 on the Billboard 200, with the singles “Modern Day Cowboy” and “Little Suzi” among the most popular. Tesla released “The Great Radio Controversy” three years later, which went double platinum and reached No. 18 on Billboard’s 200 list. “Hang Tough,” “Love Song,” “The Way It Is,” and “Paradise” all charted in the Mainstream Rock Tracks category.
