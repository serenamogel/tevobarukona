Donald Trump Jr. wife-American political activist and businessman, Donald John Trump Jr. was born on December 31, 1977.

Trump was born to Ivana and the former president of the United States of America, Donald Trump in Manhattan, New York City. He has two siblings, Ivanka and Eric and two half siblings; Tiffany and Barron.

Through his father, Donald Trump is a great-grandson of Elizabeth Christ Trump, who started the Trump Organization, and a grandson of Fred Trump.

When he was a young boy, Trump looked up to his maternal grandpa, Milo Zelnek, who lived close to Prague and enjoyed summertime activities like camping, fishing, hunting, and studying Czech.

His father’s extramarital affair led to his parents’ divorce when he was 12 years old. For a year following the divorce, Trump Jr. was hostile toward his father out of resentment for the deeds that had torn the family apart.

Trump attended the Pottstown, Pennsylvania, boarding schools Buckley School and The Hill School before attending the University of Pennsylvania’s (Penn) Wharton School, where he earned a B.S. in Economics in 2000.

Donald Trump Jr. career

Along with his younger brother Eric, Trump manages The Trump Organization as an executive vice president and trustee.

In spite of a promise that they wouldn’t, the brothers continued to do business, make investments abroad, and receive payments in the form of equity in their American assets from foreign governments while their father was president.

Trump participated on his father’s TV program The Apprentice as a boardroom judge as well. In 2019 and 2020, he wrote Triggered and Liberal Privilege, respectively.

Trump participated in his father’s 2016 campaign for president. He met with a Russian lawyer who promised to provide damaging information about Hillary Clinton’s presidential campaign as part of his collaboration with Russia in their meddling in the 2016 American elections.

Trump participated in the 2018 midterm election campaigns supporting a number of Republicans. He has supported a number of conspiracies.

Trump participated actively in his father’s 2020 presidential campaign as well, frequently appearing on the campaign trail and garnering headlines for his erroneous assertions.

He supported the idea that the election had been rigged and called for “total war” as the results were being tallied. After his father lost, he started making efforts to reverse the verdict.

He gave a speech at the rally that sparked the Capitol storm and warned Trump’s detractors that “we’re coming for you.”

In the criminal investigation into the attack, the District of Columbia’s attorney general, Karl Racine, stated in January 2021 that he is considering whether to accuse Donald Trump Jr. of encouraging the violent attack on the U.S. Capitol.

According to a CNN report from April 2022, two days after the election, Donald Trump Jr. texted White House Chief of Staff Mark Meadows describing ways to rig the Electoral College results in order to secure his father’s reelection.

On January 14, 2021, it was revealed that Donald Trump Jr. is a person of interest in the criminal probe into the misappropriation of his father’s inaugural funds in Washington, D.C., and that prosecutors plan to interview him regarding his role in “grossly overpaying” for use of event space at the Trump Hotel in Washington for the 2017 inauguration.

Who is Donald Trump Jr.’s wife?

Donald Trump Jr. was previously married to Vanessa Kay Haydon. They got married on November 12, 2005 and had five children together. However, they divorced in 2018.

Trump is currently engaged Kimberly Guilfoyle. They got engaged on December 31, 2020.
