5 SECRETS TO GETTING DINNER ON THE TABLE...FAST!

  • brownie mix – You want to look for a brownie mix that doesn’t have any mix-ins – not even chocolate chips. The reason for this is that we have to heat the brownie mix up to 165°F to make it safe to eat and if there are any mix-ins in the brownie mix, they will melt.
  • pudding mix – Chocolate pudding mix gives this edible brownie batter dip an even deeper, richer chocolate flavor and creates the creamy and smooth consistency that makes this dip so delicious. Vanilla pudding mix will work in a pinch.
  • cream cheese – Provides the creamy base for the dip and provides a counterpart to the sweetness from the brownie mix and pudding mix.
  • whipped topping – I used Cool Whip but you can of course make homemade whipped cream. It’s a little more delicate and will need to be carefully folded into the cream cheese. If using whipped cream , the dip should be enjoyed immediately.
  • milk – About a half cup of milk is needed to give this dip a dip-able consistency. Add the milk a few tablespoons at a time until you’re desired consistency is reached. I like to check with a few of my dippers to make sure they are strong enough to hold up to the dip.

How To Make Brownie Batter Dip

  • Heat the brownie mix in the microwave in 30 second increments, until it reaches 165°F to make it safe to eat raw.
  • Beat softened cream cheese until light and fluffy.
  • Add whipped topping to the cream cheese and beat until combined.
  • Gradually add the cooled brownie mix to the cream cheese mixture.
  • Add milk a few tablespoons at a time until desired consistency is achieved.
  • Refrigerate until ready to serve.

    • Storage Information

    This Brownie Batter Dip can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator in an airtight container for up to 3 days. I do not recommend freezing.

    When serving from the refrigerator, give the dip a quick stir and if it’s too thick, you can mix in another tablespoon or two of milk to thin.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Why do I have to microwave the brownie mix? Heating the mix to 165°F ensures that it is safe to eat by eliminating any potentially harmful bacteria that might be present. If you don’t have a thermometer, heat for 2 full minutes (in 30 second increments, stirring in between).

    Can I use homemade brownie mix for this recipe? Yes, homemade brownie mix can be used as long as it’s safe to consume raw.

    Is it safe to eat raw brownie mix in this dip? Yes, heating the brownie mix to 165°F ensures it’s safe to eat.

    Can I serve the edible brownie batter straight from the fridge? You can but check for consistency first. If it’s too thick you can either thin a little with milk, stir vigorously to loosen up the dip or let it sit out for a few minutes before enjoying.

    Brownie Batter Dip

    This irresistible edible Brownie Batter Dip is a chocolate lovers dream come true! Quick and easy to make with just 5 ingredients, this edible brownie batter is best served with a variety of dippers including fresh strawberries, pretzel, cookies and crackers!

    Prep Time 10 minutes minutes Servings 10 Calories 372kcal Author Trish – Mom On Timeout

    Ingredients

    • 16 to 18 ounces brownie mix 1 box
    • 3.4 ounces instant chocolate pudding mix 1 box
    • 8 ounces cream cheese softened
    • 8 ounces whipped topping thawed
    • 6 to 8 tablespoons milk I used whole milk but any milk will work

    Toppings (Optional)

    • mini chocolate chips

    Dippers

    • strawberries
    • graham crackers
    • animal crackers
    • cookies
    • pretzels
    • vanilla wafers

    Instructions

    • Transfer brownie mix to a microwave safe bowl and heat in 30 second increments on high heat, whisking in between, for 2 minutes or until the mix reaches 165°F. Let the mix cool completely before continuing.

      16 to 18 ounces brownie mix

    • Whisk the chocolate pudding mix into the cooled brownie mix.

      3.4 ounces instant chocolate pudding mix

    • In a large bowl or the bowl of a stand mixer, beat the softened cream cheese until light and fluffy. Add the whipped topping and continue beating until combined.

      8 ounces cream cheese, 8 ounces whipped topping

    • Gradually beat in the brownie mix mixture until combined and no streaks remain.

    • Add milk a few tablespoons at a time, beating well in between each addition, until desired consistency is reached.

      6 to 8 tablespoons milk

    • Transfer dip to a serving bowl and top with mini chocolate chips. Serve with your favorite dippers and enjoy!

      mini chocolate chips, strawberries, graham crackers, animal crackers, cookies, pretzels, vanilla wafers
