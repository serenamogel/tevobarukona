Biography of Stephen A Smith

Born on October 14, 1967, in the Bronx, New York City, Stephen Anthony Smith was raised in Queens’ Hollis section, alongside six siblings.

Stephen A. Smith is parents hailed from Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands, and his father managed a hardware store while his mother tended to homemaking.

Notably, Smith’s maternal grandmother was of European descent, while his other grandparents were of African heritage.

Developing a fervor for basketball early on, Smith earned a basketball scholarship to attend Winston-Salem State University in North Carolina.

Stephen A. Smith is college basketball experience was cultivated under the tutelage of Hall of Fame coach Clarence Gaines.

While an active player, Smith authored a column in the university newspaper, The News Argus, asserting that Gaines should retire due to health reasons.

In 1991, he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in mass communication and is also affiliated with the Omega Psi Phi fraternity.

Professional Journey of Stephen A Smith

Stephen A Smith embarked on his professional journey as a print journalist, contributing to various newspapers including the Winston-Salem Journal, the Greensboro News and Record, the New York Daily News, and the Philadelphia Inquirer.

Transitioning from sports reporting to a general sports columnist, he covered significant athletic events and interviewed prominent figures such as Allen Iverson, Kobe Bryant, Michael Jordan, and LeBron James.

In 2003, Smith joined ESPN, emerging as a frequent commentator and analyst on programs like SportsCenter, NFL Live, NBA Shootaround, and Pardon the Interruption.

The Stephen A. Smith Show, his radio broadcast, debuted on ESPN Radio.

Furthering his influence, he presided over Quite Frankly with Stephen A. Smith, a nightly talk show featuring discussions with celebrities and athletes.

A sojourn from ESPN in 2007 saw Smith engage with other radio and television avenues, including roles with Fox Sports Radio and Sirius XM Radio, and guest appearances on CNN and entertainment productions like I Think I Love My Wife (2007), General Hospital (2007), and more.

Stephen A. Smith’s reintegration with ESPN in 2012 was marked by his appointment as a full-time analyst and commentator.

He became a co-host of First Take, partnering with Skip Bayless initially and later Max Kellerman, for spirited debates.

He also reclaimed his mantle as an NBA analyst for ESPN’s NBA Countdown and broadcasts, complementing his original content on ESPN+ in the form of Stephen A’s World, a show presenting a distinctive blend of sports news and culture.
